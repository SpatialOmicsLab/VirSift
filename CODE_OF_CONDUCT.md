# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the VirSift project a respectful,
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or technical
background.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive project environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints, experiences, and technical backgrounds
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing criticism on ideas, code, documentation, or evidence rather than people
- Showing empathy toward contributors and users
- Protecting private, restricted, and sensitive information

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or intimidation
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- Publishing another person's private information without permission
- Posting restricted sequence data, credentials, or sensitive surveillance metadata
- Deliberate disruption of project discussions or collaboration
- Conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional or scientific setting

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards.
They may edit, remove, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests,
and other contributions that are inconsistent with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when
behavior is harmful to the project or its community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all VirSift project spaces, including issues,
pull requests, discussions, reviews, documentation, meetings, and other public
or private channels used for project collaboration. It also applies when an
individual is publicly representing the project.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Possible actions include:

1. **Correction** — a private explanation and request to correct the behavior.
2. **Warning** — a formal warning with consequences for continued behavior.
3. **Temporary restriction** — temporary exclusion from project interaction.
4. **Permanent restriction** — permanent exclusion from project participation.

The response will be proportionate to the seriousness, frequency, and impact of
the conduct.
